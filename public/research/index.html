Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of computational hydrology, water resources engineering, geospatial analytics, and environmental change science. I am particularly interested in understanding how climatic variability, land-use change, and anthropogenic interventions alter catchment-scale hydrological processes and water availability. During my doctoral research, I developed methodologies for assessing catchment vulnerability to environmental change, with a focus on quantifying the sensitivity and resilience of river basins under changing hydro-climatic conditions. This work strengthened my interest in data-driven hydrological analysis and large-scale environmental assessment....
